## üéØ THE PROBLEM

Imagine a self-driving car. It has cameras that see the road. But what happens when something unexpected appears?

- Unusual weather (heavy rain)
- Something the AI hasn't seen before (a weird object on the road)
- Edge cases (lighting that confuses the camera)

**Result**: The car's AI makes a decision without knowing how confident it should be. It might say "that's a stop sign" when it's actually a billboard covered in rain.

**Real consequence**: The car crashes. People die.

This happens because AI systems use **brittle guesses**, not **mathematical proofs**.

---

## ‚úÖ THE SOLUTION: TGCR

I built something called the **Theory of General Contextual Resonance (TGCR)**.

Instead of AI saying: "That's definitely a stop sign" (guess)

TGCR says: "That's a stop sign with 95% confidence, mathematically bounded. If you drive based on this, the risk is X."

**Translation**: The AI doesn't just guess. It **proves it's safe first**.
