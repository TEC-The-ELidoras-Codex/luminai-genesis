## **Criticism 2: "The thermodynamic derivation is not rigorous."**

**Response:** **Agreed.** I use thermodynamic reasoning as an **analogy**, 
not a rigorous derivation. The exact threshold value (0.7 vs 0.65 vs 0.8) 
is an **empirical question**, not a theoretical prediction.

**What's needed:** Independent validation of whether a threshold exists, 
and if so, where it is located. The **existence of a threshold is more 
fundamental than its precise location.**

**Status:** I state this explicitly in Section 4 ("Thermodynamic Analogy 
- Not Rigorous Derivation").

**Response:** **Testable.** This is Alternative Explanation #4 in the 
paper. I propose developing SAR-Neutral (non-crisis prompts) to test 
whether SAR and SAR-N correlate.

**Prediction 6:** If SAR and SAR-Neutral don't correlate (r < 0.3), 
the framework is domain-specific, not general.

**Status:** This is a falsification criterion. If someone tests this 
and finds no correlation, I will revise the framework.

## **Criticism 6: "You're making claims about consciousness."**

**Response:** **No, I'm not.** I explicitly state in Section 8 (Confidence 
Levels) that "W-score predicts consciousness" has **low confidence (<30%)** 
and is **highly speculative.**

The framework is about **coherence**, not consciousness. The convergence 
with Kyle Fish's consciousness estimate is **interesting** but not 
**definitive**.

**Status:** I am not claiming to have solved consciousness. I am proposing 
a coherence metric that may or may not relate to it.
