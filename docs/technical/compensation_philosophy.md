# COMPENSATION PHILOSOPHY

## LUMINAI TECHNOLOGIES — Living Wage+, Generational Investment

**Purpose:** Clearly articulate compensation values and mechanics that align LuminAI's mission with equitable pay, staff wellbeing, and long-term wealth building for employees.

**Scope:** Applies to all employees (full-time, part-time, and contractors where contract explicitly references this policy) of LuminAI Technologies LLC.

**Version:** 1.0 — December 11, 2025

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES

- **Living Wage+**: Base pay meets or exceeds local living wage plus a 10% premium.
- **Transparency**: Salary bands by role and level published internally.
- **Equity & Inclusion**: Pay practices reduce bias and promote representation.
- **Generational Investment**: Company contributes to employee long-term wealth (debt reduction, matched savings, or equity shares depending on role).
- **Sustainability**: Compensation scales with company financial health and mission priorities.

---

## SALARY STRUCTURE

### Base Pay

1. **Living Wage Benchmarking**

   - LuminAI adopts a living wage benchmark per region (MIT Living Wage Calculator or local equivalent).
   - Base pay = Greater of (market median for role, region living wage × 1.10).

2. **Banded Structure**

   - Roles grouped into bands (IC1, IC2, IC3; M1, M2; Exec)
   - Each band has a published min/median/max tied to market data

3. **Cost-of-Living Adjustments (COLA)**
   - Annual COLA applied based on local inflation indices

### Bonuses & Variable Pay

1. **Mission Bonus Pool**

   - Up to 10% of payroll reserved annually for mission bonuses (distributed for exceptional impact on product, ethics, or community outcomes)

2. **Performance Bonus**

   - Individual bonus target 5-15% depending on level; awarded based on transparent goals and calibrated peer review

3. **Profit Donation Sharing**
   - Company donates 25% of net profit to the Foundation; a portion (up to 2% of net profit) is earmarked for an Employee Impact Grant that employees may apply to as teams

### Benefits & Total Rewards

1. **Health & Wellness**: Employer-sponsored health plans with employer covering at least 80% of premiums for employees.
2. **Retirement & Savings**: Employer match to retirement accounts up to 4% of salary.
3. **Paid Time Off**: 20 days PTO + 10 public holidays + flexible recharge days.
4. **Parental Leave**: 16 weeks fully paid for primary caregivers; 6 weeks for secondary caregivers.
5. **Professional Development**: $3,000 annual learning stipend per employee.

---

## GENERATIONAL INVESTMENT OPTIONS

1. **Education Match** (Legacy Scholars complement)

   - Company match to employee education savings plans up to $1,000/year

2. **Employee Equity**

   - Equity grants for key hires and above (policy governed by separate Equity Plan)

3. **Debt Relief Program (Pilot)**

   - Pilot program for student loan repayment assistance: up to $3,000/year per employee

4. **Homeownership Assistance (Future Program)**
   - Explore low-interest loan / matched savings for down-payment assistance as program matures

---

## PAY EQUITY & TRANSPARENCY

1. **Salary Bands Posted Internally**: Each position's band min/med/max published on internal HR portal.
2. **Compensation Reviews**: Twice-yearly calibration meetings to review equity and address underpayment.
3. **External Audit**: Compensation equity audit annually with public summary of findings (anonymized).

---

## ADJUSTMENTS, RAISES, AND PROMOTIONS

1. **Merit Increases**: Awarded following performance reviews and calibrated against band ranges.
2. **Promotion Increases**: Minimum 8-12% bump depending on band differential.
3. **Market Adjustments**: Conducted quarterly for fast-moving disciplines; immediate adjustments when markets materially shift.

---

## SPECIAL CASES

### Contractors

Contractors engaged for >12 months are eligible for a pro-rated professional development stipend and are reviewed for conversion to FTE roles.

### Remote & International Employees

Pay is adjusted by region using a published geographic multiplier; employees may opt into equivalency pay when relocating.

### Salary Confidentiality

Employees are encouraged to discuss compensation; retaliation for sharing salary information is prohibited.

---

## GOVERNANCE

1. **Compensation Committee**: Comprised of CEO, CFO, Head of People, and 1 independent board member.
2. **Approval Authority**: Committee approves all executive compensation and equity grants.
3. **Documentation**: All compensation decisions recorded with rationale and approval sign-offs.

---

## IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

**Month 0-3:** Publish bands, set up payroll living-wage calculations, roll out health benefits.

**Month 3-6:** Run first compensation calibration; pilot debt-relief program.

**Month 6-12:** Conduct external equity audit; publish anonymized summary.

---

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Will I lose pay if I relocate?**  
A: Pay may be adjusted according to published regional multipliers; employees can opt into equivalency.

**Q: How are raises decided?**  
A: Through performance reviews, market data, and band positioning—transparent and calibrated.

**Q: Is my salary public?**  
A: Salary bands are internal. Individual salaries are private, but employees may share them freely.
