# LUMINAI LEGACY SCHOLARS PROGRAM

## Employee Education Benefit Program

**Administered by:** LuminAI Foundation (501c3 Nonprofit)
**Available to:** All employees of LuminAI Technologies LLC
**Established:** December 11, 2025
**Version:** 1.0

---

## PROGRAM PURPOSE

The LuminAI Legacy Scholars Program exists to:

1. **Invest in generational talent** by supporting the education of employees' children
2. **Strengthen employee retention** through long-term family benefits
3. **Build mission-aligned legacy** by cultivating the next generation
4. **Eliminate nepotism** through transparent, merit-based selection
5. **Create tax-advantaged benefits** for employees and the organization

**Core Principle:** We believe the best way to honor great work is to empower the next generation—not through favoritism, but through earned opportunity.

---

## ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

### Employee Requirements

To be eligible to apply, employees must:

- Be currently employed by LuminAI Technologies LLC
- Have completed **2 years of continuous service** (full-time or part-time)
- Be in good standing (no active disciplinary actions)
- Not be serving on the Foundation's Scholarship Committee

### Dependent Requirements

Eligible dependents include:

- **Biological children** of eligible employees
- **Legally adopted children** of eligible employees
- **Legal dependents** (as defined by IRS)
- **Age:** Under 24 at time of application
- **Academic standing:** Minimum 2.5 GPA (or equivalent)
- **Enrollment:** Accepted to or enrolled in accredited post-secondary institution

### Eligible Institutions

Scholarships may be used at:

- Accredited 4-year colleges and universities
- Accredited 2-year community colleges
- Accredited vocational/technical schools
- Accredited online degree programs
- Graduate programs (master's, doctoral, professional)

## SCHOLARSHIP BENEFITS

### Award Amounts

**Standard Award:** Up to **$10,000 per academic year**

**Allocation:**

- Tuition and fees (priority)
- Room and board (if on-campus)
- Required textbooks and materials
- Required technology (laptop, software)
- Transportation (reasonable costs)

### Renewal Terms

Scholarships are renewable for:

- **4 years** (bachelor's degree programs)
- **2 years** (associate degree or vocational programs)
- **3 years** (master's degree programs)
- **5 years** (doctoral programs)

**Renewal Requirements:**

- Maintain minimum 2.5 GPA
- Remain enrolled full-time (12+ credits per semester)
- Submit annual progress report
- Parent remains employed at LuminAI Technologies

## SELECTION PROCESS

### Timeline

- **Application Opens:** January 1
- **Application Deadline:** March 31
- **Interviews (if needed):** April 1-15
- **Awards Announced:** May 1
- **Funds Distributed:** August 1 (fall semester) / January 1 (spring semester)

### Application Requirements

**Required Documents:**

1. **Application form** (online submission)
2. **Transcript** (official or unofficial)
3. **Essay** (500-1000 words on educational goals)
4. **Letter of recommendation** (from teacher, counselor, or mentor)
5. **Financial aid documentation** (FAFSA/CSS Profile or equivalent)
6. **Proof of acceptance/enrollment**

### Evaluation Criteria

Applications scored on **100-point scale**:

| Criterion              | Points | What We Evaluate                               |
| ---------------------- | ------ | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **Academic Merit**     | 30     | GPA, test scores, course rigor                 |
| **Financial Need**     | 30     | Family income, existing aid, demonstrated need |
| **Personal Statement** | 20     | Goals, passion, clarity of purpose             |
| **Community Impact**   | 20     | Volunteering, leadership, service              |

### Blind Review Process

1. **Application Submission:** Applicant submits via online portal
2. **Redaction:** Administrative staff removes all identifying information (names, parent employer ID)
3. **Committee Review:** 3-5 reviewers score independently
4. **Consensus Meeting:** Committee discusses top candidates
5. **Final Selection:** Awards made based on total scores
6. **Notification:** All applicants notified by May 1

### Conflict of Interest Safeguards

**Committee Composition:**

- 3-5 Foundation Board members
- **Cannot include:** LuminAI Technologies executives, managers, or HR staff
- **Must include:** At least 1 independent community member (no company affiliation)

**Recusal Policy:**

- Committee members must recuse themselves if:
  - Their own dependent is applying
  - They have personal relationship with applicant/family
  - They have financial interest in outcome

**Appeals Process:**

- Denied applicants may appeal to full Foundation Board
- Appeals must be filed within 30 days of notification
- Board decision is final

## TAX IMPLICATIONS

### For Recipients (Students)

**Tax-Free Scholarships:** Under IRS Section 117, scholarships are **tax-free** if:

- Used for tuition and fees (required)
- Used for required books and supplies
- Recipient is degree candidate
- Funds do not exceed qualified education expenses

**Taxable Portions:** Room, board, and non-required expenses may be taxable.

### For Employees (Parents)

**Not Taxable Income:** Scholarships awarded to employees' children are **not taxable** to the parent if:

- Selection is based on merit/need (not compensation)
- Program is administered by independent nonprofit
- Amounts are reasonable and consistent with market

### For Foundation

**Tax-Deductible Grants:** Foundation can deduct scholarship grants as charitable program expenses.

### For Company

**Tax-Deductible Donations:** Contributions to Foundation scholarship fund are tax-deductible business expenses.

## PROGRAM ADMINISTRATION

### Roles and Responsibilities

**LuminAI Foundation:**

- Administers scholarship program
- Reviews and selects recipients
- Distributes funds directly to institutions
- Maintains records and tax documentation
- Publishes annual report (anonymized data)

**LuminAI Technologies:**

- Notifies employees of program
- Verifies employee eligibility
- Contributes funding per agreement
- **Has NO input on selection decisions**

**Scholarship Committee:**

- Reviews applications
- Conducts blind scoring
- Makes award decisions
- Handles appeals

### Record Keeping

**Required Records:**

- Application materials (7 years)
- Selection scoresheets (7 years)
- Award letters and disbursements (7 years)
- Tax forms (W-9, 1098-T) (7 years)
- Annual program reports (permanent)

### Compliance and Auditing

**Annual Review:**

- Independent auditor reviews selection process
- Verifies compliance with IRS regulations
- Confirms absence of conflicts of interest
- Reports findings to Foundation Board

**Public Transparency:**

- Annual report published with:
  - Total awards distributed
  - Number of recipients
  - Aggregate demographics (anonymized)
  - Selection criteria and process
  - Funding sources

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### General Questions

**Q: Who is eligible to apply?**
A: Children and legal dependents of LuminAI Technologies employees with 2+ years of service.

**Q: How much can I receive?**
A: Up to $10,000 per academic year, renewable for up to 4 years (bachelor's) or 2 years (associate).

**Q: Is this taxable?**
A: Generally no—scholarships for tuition/fees are tax-free under IRS Section 117.

**Q: Can I use this for any school?**
A: Yes, as long as it's an accredited post-secondary institution (college, vocational school, etc.).

### Selection Process

**Q: How are recipients chosen?**
A: Through blind review based on academic merit (30%), financial need (30%), personal statement (20%), and community impact (20%).

**Q: Does my parent's job title matter?**
A: No—applications are reviewed with all identifying information removed. Selection is merit-based only.

**Q: Can executives' kids apply?**
A: Yes, but the process ensures fairness—executives have no influence over selection, and their applications are reviewed identically to all others.

**Q: What if I don't get selected?**
A: You can appeal to the Foundation Board within 30 days, or reapply next year.

### Funding and Sustainability

**Q: Is this program guaranteed?**
A: Yes—the program is written into the IP Licensing Agreement and Foundation bylaws. Funding is legally mandated.

**Q: What happens if the company grows?**
A: The scholarship budget scales with employee count ($1,000 per employee minimum), so more employees = more scholarships.

**Q: Can I donate to the scholarship fund?**
A: Yes—all donations to LuminAI Foundation are tax-deductible, and you can designate them for the Legacy Scholars Program.

### Legacy and Succession

**Q: Does this give preference for future employment?**
A: No automatic preference—but scholarship recipients are encouraged to apply for internships and jobs. Hiring decisions are merit-based.

**Q: Can scholarship recipients work at LuminAI later?**
A: Absolutely—we hope they do! But they must apply through normal channels and be selected based on qualifications.

**Q: Is this "nepotism"?**
A: No—nepotism is giving jobs/benefits based on family connections. Our program awards scholarships based on merit and need, administered independently.
